Dan Magill Tennis Complex

The Dan Magill Tennis Complex has long stood as a beacon of excellence in collegiate tennis. With a total of 16 courts (12 outdoor and four indoor) and a total seating capacity of more than 5,000 fans, it is one of the largest on-campus tennis facilities in the country. The ever-evolving complex underwent a $7.5 million development and renovation project in 2002 that included the construction of brand-new clubhouses for the men's and women's programs. In 1991, the complex received lights thanks to the generous support of Oscar-winning actress and Athens native, Kim Basinger.

The Dan Magill Tennis Complex is often regarded as the nicest on-campus tennis center in the United States. Therefore, it is no surprise that the University of Georgia has hosted the NCAA National Championship a total of thirty-two times. Twenty-four of these were men's, three were women's, and four were both genders in the same year. Five of the UGA Men's Team's six NCAA National Championships were won at the Dan Magill Tennis Complex. One of the UGA Women's Team's two NCAA National Championship titles were won at home.

2017-2018 Schedule

Aside from the fall, the season officially kicks off on January 28 and 29 against Iowa, Arkansas, and Memphis. These matches will be played in Athens. The team's first official SEC match is against Florida in Gainesville. Our first home SEC match is against Texas A&M on March 11. Personally, I am most excited about the Auburn and Vanderbilt matches. I hate Auburn after they beat our football team this past weekend, so I would love to watch us beat them in every other sport. Vanderbilt recruited two of the top five players in the nation last year, as well as some very stout foreign talent. I am really looking forward to seeing how good these freshman really are. The SEC tournament is held in Tuscaloosa on April 26 through April 29. The first rounds of the NCAA Championship tournament begin on May 12. Following that, the Finals are held May 18 through May 28 in Winston-Salem, NC. I look forward to seeing the Dawgs take the trophy for the seventh time. Go Dawgs!
